Historical Foundations

Principle in Genesis

From the beginning, God established the principle of separation between the sacred and the common. The tree of the knowledge of good and evil was set apart for God, teaching us that there are things that belong exclusively to the Lord.

"But of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat..." - Genesis 2:17

Abraham - The First Tithe

The first biblical account of tithing was when Abraham gave a tenth part of everything to Melchizedek, king of Salem and priest of the Most High God. This act demonstrated recognition and reverence to God.

"And he gave him a tithe of all." - Genesis 14:20

Question for Reflection

Should we return the tithe today?

This is a fundamental question that every Christian should carefully consider, seeking understanding in the Scriptures and guidance from the Holy Spirit for a response that honors God and reflects true Christian stewardship.

Frequently Asked Questions

While the New Covenant does not impose tithing as a law, the principle of returning to God a portion of our resources remains. Jesus mentioned tithing without revoking it (Matthew 23:23), and the apostle Paul taught about systematic generosity (1 Corinthians 16:2). Tithing is a biblical starting point, but Christian generosity should go beyond, according to each person's possibilities.

The Bible speaks about firstfruits (Proverbs 3:9) and giving according to prosperity (1 Corinthians 16:2). Traditionally, it is understood that tithes should be calculated on the gross amount, before deductions, as this demonstrates that God has priority in our finances. However, the most important thing is to have a heart willing to honor God with our resources.

Jesus taught that we should seek first the Kingdom of God, and all other things will be added to us (Matthew 6:33). If you are in debt, it is important to honor God with your tithes and offerings, trusting that He will provide. At the same time, it is wise to seek financial guidance and develop a plan to get out of debt. Many testify that by being faithful in tithes even in difficult situations, they experienced God's supernatural movement in their finances.

Malachi 3:10 instructs us to bring the tithes to the "storehouse," which in practice was the temple, the place of support for the Levites and the sacred service. The tithe has the specific purpose of supporting God's work through the local church. However, voluntary offerings can and should be directed to social and missionary works, as God directs each person. The important thing is that both the tithe and the offerings are given with joy and purpose (2 Corinthians 9:7).
