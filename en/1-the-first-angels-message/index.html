The Everlasting Gospel

What is the Gospel?

Gospel means "good news". It is the message of salvation that God offers to humanity through Jesus Christ.

üì¢ The Gospel of the Kingdom

"And this gospel of the kingdom shall be preached in all the world for a witness unto all nations; and then shall the end come."

Matthew 24:14
‚ú® The "Gospel of the Kingdom" refers to the good news about God's government and the authority of Jesus Christ, which must be proclaimed to all before His return.

"For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ: for it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth; to the Jew first, and also to the Greek." Romans 1:16

üö™ The Door to the Kingdom

But to enter the Kingdom, there is an exclusive "Door". No one can enter by their own merits, but only through Jesus Christ and faith in His sacrifice.

"Moreover, brethren, I declare unto you the gospel which I preached unto you... how that Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; And that he was buried, and that he rose again the third day according to the scriptures."

1 Corinthians 15:1-4

üí™ The Transforming Power of the Gospel

üî• Power to Save

The gospel is not just information, but the divine power itself in action to transform lives.

üåç For Every Person

Jews and Gentiles, every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people (Revelation 14:6).

‚úùÔ∏è The Plan of Salvation

1. Christ's Substitutive Death

Jesus died for our sins, paying the price that we should have paid.

"Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures" - 1 Corinthians 15:3

2. Teaching Obedience

Christ not only died for us but also teaches us how to live in obedience to God.

"If ye love me, keep my commandments" - John 14:15

"He that hath my commandments, and keepeth them, he it is that loveth me: and he that loveth me shall be loved of my Father, and I will love him, and will manifest myself to him" - John 14:21

3. The Ten Commandments as a Standard

God's law remains the standard of righteousness for the saved.

"Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we establish the law" - Romans 3:31

"What shall we say then? Shall we continue in sin, that grace may abound? God forbid. How shall we, that are dead to sin, live any longer therein?" - Romans 6:1,2

üí° Grace is not freedom to sin. Studying and obeying the Word brings us out of darkness.

Why "Everlasting Gospel"?

‚ú® Eternal Plan

The plan of salvation was established before the foundation of the world (Ephesians 1:4).

üåç For All Times

The message of salvation is valid for every era and generation.

‚ôæÔ∏è Eternal Effects

The salvation offered results in eternal life with God.

üéØ Final Message

It is the last message of mercy before the final judgment.

üíé Important Conclusion:

To fear God is to receive Christ! Jesus is God's own wisdom. When we accept Him as Savior and Lord, we are truly fearing God.

üåü Result of the Fear of the Lord

Is it possible to keep God's Law today?

The Pharisees and doctors of the Law in Jesus' day were extremely zealous in keeping the commandments, but they failed to fulfill the Law. Why? Because they tried to do it through their own human efforts.

"For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ: for it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth..."

Romans 1:16

We cannot keep God's commandments by ourselves. We need God's power in our lives. Notice that the commandments are almost all in the future indicative, indicating a promise: those who accept salvation in Jesus and receive the power of the Holy Spirit will begin to obey.

"I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me."

Philippians 4:13

This is how the new covenant mentioned in Hebrews 8 is fulfilled, where God promised:

"I will put my laws into their mind, and write them in their hearts: and I will be to them a God, and they shall be to me a people."

Hebrews 8:10

Obedience is the natural result of a heart transformed by the power of the gospel, not the condition for salvation.

The Summary of the Law

Jesus summarized the Ten Commandments into two great principles:

"Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind."

Matthew 22:37

"Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself."

Matthew 22:39

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Summarizing is not the same as reducing. A summary contains the essence of the entire original content, while reducing would mean eliminating parts. Jesus did not eliminate the commandments but showed that Love is the foundation of them all (Matthew 22:40).

Note: God's Law was not abolished by Christ but rather fulfilled and exalted by Him (Matthew 5:17-19).

How to Give Glory to God?

The Bible teaches us that there are three main ways to give glory to God:

1st Way: Praise and Gratitude

Recognize God as the Source of Everything

"Whoso offereth praise glorifieth me: and to him that ordereth his conversation aright will I shew the salvation of God."

Psalm 50:23

"Give unto the LORD the glory due unto his name: bring an offering, and come before him: worship the LORD in the beauty of holiness."

1 Chronicles 16:29

"Enter into his gates with thanksgiving, and into his courts with praise: be thankful unto him, and bless his name."

Psalm 100:4

"In every thing give thanks: for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus concerning you."

1 Thessalonians 5:18

üíù Practical Examples:

  • Thank God for health, family, and provisions.
  • Recognize God as the source of all blessings.
  • Praise Him even in difficulties.
  • Give glory to God publicly.

2nd Way: Confession

Confess and Repent of Sins

"My son, give, I pray thee, glory to the LORD God of Israel, and make confession unto him..."

Joshua 7:19

üíé God's Character in Confession

"The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth, Keeping mercy for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, and that will by no means clear the guilty..."

Exodus 34:6,7

‚öñÔ∏è God is merciful AND just, love AND justice. He is all of this! Confessing is attributing the guilt to myself, recognizing that I sinned.

üò¢ Godly Sorrow

"For godly sorrow worketh repentance to salvation not to be repented of: but the sorrow of the world worketh death."

2 Corinthians 7:10

üíî Be ashamed of sin: Be grieved, not desiring or wanting to do it again. This is the sorrow that leads to salvation!

üíß Steps of True Confession:

  • Recognize: Identify and name the specific sin.
  • Acknowledge Guilt: Take full responsibility.
  • Feel Godly Sorrow: Be truly ashamed and grieved.
  • Forsake: No longer desire or want to do it again.
  • Seek Forgiveness: Trust in God's mercy.

3rd Way: Fruits of Faith

Good Works that Manifest the Light

"Herein is my Father glorified, that ye bear much fruit; so shall ye be my disciples."

John 15:8

"Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven."

Matthew 5:16

"For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus unto good works, which God hath before ordained that we should walk in them."

Ephesians 2:10

"The light of the righteous rejoiceth: but the lamp of the wicked shall be put out."

Proverbs 13:9

üå± Works that Glorify:

  • Live out Christ's character daily.
  • Reflect God's light through conduct.
  • Practice the good works of God's Law.

‚ú® The Result of Giving Glory

"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness." 1 John 1:9

Why has the Hour of Judgment Come?

The Prophecy of the 2300 Evenings and Mornings

Reminder: This prophecy will be studied in detail in a future lesson, where we will explore its historical fulfillment and prophetic meaning.

"And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed."

Daniel 8:14

üìä Prophetic Interpretation

Start: 457 B.C.

Artaxerxes' decree to rebuild Jerusalem.

Duration: 2300 years

Applying the day-year principle (Ezekiel 4:6).

End: 1844 A.D.

Start of the investigative judgment in heaven.

‚öñÔ∏è The Investigative Judgment

Investigation:

God examines the records of every life.

"For God shall bring every work into judgment, with every secret thing, whether it be good, or whether it be evil." - Ecclesiastes 12:14
Criterion:

The Ten Commandments (James 2:10-12).

Decision:

Determines who will receive eternal life.

"But he that shall endure unto the end, the same shall be saved." - Matthew 24:13

Determined Time

God established a specific time for the start of the final judgment.

Just Judgment

Each case is analyzed individually with justice and mercy.

Urgency

This is why the message needs to urgently reach everyone.

Summary of Main Points

Universal Judgment

God will judge the actions, thoughts, and motives of all people.

Divine Law

The rule of judgment will be His law, containing the Ten Commandments.

Our Condition

Before it, we all see ourselves condemned.

Our Hope

Jesus is our only hope for forgiveness of sins.

Overcome Sin

God gives us power to overcome sin and obey His law as He desires.

Approval

By accepting Him as Savior, we will be approved in the judgment.

Didactic Flow of the Study

1

Introduction - The Universal Message

Present Revelation 14:6,7 as the only biblical message intended for all people on Earth.

Explain: What the gospel is and why it's "everlasting".

2

What is Fearing God?

Explain through Ecclesiastes 12:13,14 and Proverbs that fearing God is keeping the commandments.

Include: Reverence, obedience, wisdom, care with sin.

3

God's Law

Present the Ten Commandments as the rule of judgment (Exodus 20:3-17).

Emphasize: We are all condemned before the law.

4

How to Give Glory to God

Show the three ways: Praise/Gratitude, Confession, and Fruits of Faith (Good Works).

Verses: Ps. 50:23, Joshua 7:19, Mat. 5:16, John 15:8

5

Why has the Hour of Judgment Come?

Explain the prophecy of Daniel 8:14 (2300 evenings and mornings) and the investigative judgment.

Chronology: 457 B.C. to 1844 A.D. - Urgency of the message.

6

The Solution in Jesus

Present Jesus as forgiveness (1 John 1:9) and strength (Philippians 4:13).

Result: Approval in judgment and eternal life.
